ELIZA

ELIZA is one of the earliest chatbots to exist, where it was developed by Joseph Weizenbaum at MIT in the mid-1960s. The intention for the creation of this program was to explore and demonstrate communication between humans and machines by using pattern matching and substitution technology. Since the program was limited by its fairly simple scripts of codes, a lot of amateur coders played around creating different variations of the original chatbot. The most well known variation was called DOCTOR, which was made to respond and interact like a Rogerian psychotherapist by turning the questions back to the patient to reflect on their questions.

I found this chatbot interesting because this is the first artificial intelligence chatbot to ever exist that could somewhat mimic human conversation, and it actually successfully helped some people psychologically and even the creator Weizenbaum wrote "I had not realized ... that extremely short exposures to a relatively simple computer program could induce powerful delusional thinking in quite normal people" (Weizenbaum 7).

GERTY

GERTY is a chatbot that appears in the movie Moon. Moon is a science fiction film about the protagonist Sam nearly approaching the end of his three year contract working alone in a facility on the moon. He also has a personal assistant called GERTY to talk to provided by the company Sam works for, which is an AI that works to assist Sam from the difficulties that he faces. One day, Sam crashes his rover turning him unconscious, but he then wakes up in the base infirmary without any memory of the accident, while GERTY assists him and tells him that a rescue team is on their way. However, Sam ignores GERTY’s warnings and decides to go outside and finds another version of himself inside the rover that crashed. Sam wonders if it’s a clone of himself, but it is revealed by GERTY that both Sams were clones, and that he was the one who implanted Sam’s memory to the new clone. Both dismayed, the older Sam starts to discover the company’s secrets, where he accesses the records of the past clones who all became dysfunctional after the three year contract with the help of GERTY and a secret vault that stores a lot of Sam’s clones, which meant that the corporation has been using Sam’s clones for the entire time created just to perform the same work every three years to save costs for another worker, while acting as if they were sending him back to earth after the contract. Later, the two Sams decide that the newer Sam goes back to earth with the incoming rescue team, while the older Sam goes back to the accident site, and rebooted GERTY to wipe the records of these events since GERTY advised them to do so.


I found this movie very interesting especially because of the big twist in the plot and the AI assistant GERTY not going against humans but instead helping Sam and even revealing the corporation’s secrets even though the corporation doesn’t want Sam to know about their corrupted secrets, and how a lot of other science fiction works tend to have AI going against humans.

Siri

Siri is a digital assistant chatbot on Apple devices that uses natural language UI and is often considered the first widely adopted voice assistant on phones. While Siri was originally released as an iOS app in February 2010, Apple acquired it two months after its release and integrated it as an official feature of the iPhone 4S with its release on October 4th, 2011. Ever since officially becoming part of the iPhone 4S, Siri became an essential part of other Apple Products including the iPhone models released in the future, and it has been adapted into other hardware products from Apple such as the iPad, iPod Touch, Mac, AirPods, Apple TV, HomePod, and Apple Vision Pro. Some of the features included in this chatbot include sending messages, making calls and reminders, searching for things using the web, and all others just through the use of voice commands, eliminating the need for manual input such as navigating through menus.

I found this chatbot interesting because this form of chatbot ultimately caused a huge transition from traditional command-based interactions to human-like conversational forms of AIs with way more intelligence than ever. I also found it fascinating how this technology expanded the adoption of AI in the general public.


Tay

Tay is an AI chatbot on Twitter developed by Microsoft’s programmers back in 2016 to appeal to the millennials and understand human conversations by interacting with them and learning through other users’ posts. While it seemed to be an interesting idea with Microsoft claiming the more you talk to her the smarter she becomes, Microsoft had to quickly shut down Tay because she was tweeting the most horrific racial slurs on the platform in less than a day after its release, as the AI system lacked judgment in morality and a subset of people on the platform decided to abuse that by interacting with the chatbot using hateful comments, which quickly led to Tay tweeting offensive and hurtful tweets.

I found this chatbot very interesting because this incident reveals how us humans should be extremely careful with how much power we’re giving to artificial intelligence. This also reminds us the AI can quickly absorb and replicate the bad aspects of human behavior especially when the chatbot is unregulated as such.

My perspectives on AI

Is AI a fear for humanity?

I believe AI is not something to be feared or worried about because it rather represents how technology has evolved over the last few decades, and it should instead be used as a rapidly evolving tool that we can take advantage of and improve our lives from it.

Are current AIs actually sophisticated enough?

While AIs have evolved significantly thoughout the recent years, I still think they have room for a lot of improvements because current AI chatbots still tend to struggle with understanding the user's input, and they still fall significantly behind human intelligence, meaning that they will most likely remain as tools that will help humans instead of completely wiping the necessity of human work.
